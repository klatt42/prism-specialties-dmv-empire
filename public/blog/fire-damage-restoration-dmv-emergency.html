Types of Fire and Smoke Damage We Restore

💨 Smoke Damage

Wet Smoke: Low-heat fires create sticky, smearing residues that penetrate deeply into porous materials.

Dry Smoke: High-heat fires create fine, powdery residues that spread throughout the structure.

Protein Smoke: Kitchen fires create nearly invisible but strong-odor residues.

Our Solution: Thermal fogging, ozone treatment, and specialized cleaning compounds.

⚫ Soot Damage

Surfaces Affected: Walls, ceilings, electronics, HVAC systems, personal belongings.

Challenges: Soot particles are acidic and continue causing damage until properly removed.

Our Solution: Specialized HEPA vacuuming, chemical sponges, and ultrasonic cleaning.

🏗️ Structural Damage

Heat Damage: Warped materials, compromised structural integrity, electrical damage.

Water Damage: Firefighting efforts often cause secondary water damage.

Our Solution: Structural assessment, controlled demolition, and reconstruction.

📦 Contents Damage

Restorable: Furniture, electronics, documents, textiles, artwork.

Techniques: Ultrasonic cleaning, freeze-drying, ozone treatment.

Our Solution: Pack-out services, climate-controlled restoration facility.

Preventing Secondary Damage After Fire

Fire creates immediate damage, but secondary damage from smoke, soot, and corrosion continues for weeks without proper intervention. Our emergency stabilization prevents:

  • Acid Etching - Soot particles are acidic and permanently etch surfaces
  • Odor Penetration - Smoke odors penetrate deeper into materials over time
  • Corrosion Damage - Metals corrode rapidly in post-fire environments
  • Mold Growth - Water from firefighting efforts can lead to mold problems
  • HVAC Contamination - Smoke particles circulate throughout ventilation systems
