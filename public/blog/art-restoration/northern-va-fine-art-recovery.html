Phase 3: Active Restoration (Weeks 2-8)

The restoration work required specialized techniques rarely used outside major museums:

🔧 Canvas Reattachment

Using archival adhesives, we reattached separated canvas sections while preserving the original stretcher bars where possible. Two portraits required complete restretching on period-appropriate pine stretchers.

🎨 Paint Consolidation

Loose paint layers were consolidated using reversible restoration adhesives. This process took over 40 hours per painting, with each application requiring 24-hour curing time.

✨ Inpainting and Retouching

Areas of paint loss were carefully inpainted using reversible restoration pigments matched to the original palette. Our color matching process ensures invisible repairs under normal viewing conditions.

🛡️ Protective Finishing

Each painting received a protective varnish layer appropriate to its period and style. The Virginia landscape received a matte finish matching the artist's original intent, while the portraits received period-appropriate semi-gloss finish.
