When your Washington DC family's precious textiles are damaged, you need restoration specialists who understand that some fabrics hold memories that insurance can't measure - they can only be restored.

"My grandmother's wedding dress was destroyed in our Adams Morgan basement flood. I thought it was gone forever."

A homeowner from Adams Morgan called us in tears last spring. Three generations of women had worn her grandmother's 1940s silk wedding dress - her grandmother, her mother, and the homeowner herself. When their Victorian rowhouse basement flooded during the heavy rains, the dress had been submerged for hours in muddy water.

The insurance adjuster took one look and said "total loss." But the homeowner refused to give up on three generations of family history. She found us through a Georgetown neighbor who'd had her baptism gown restored after a house fire.

The Result: After 6 weeks of meticulous restoration, the homeowner's daughter wore the same dress her great-great-grandmother had worn in 1943. Four generations, one dress, one family legacy preserved.
