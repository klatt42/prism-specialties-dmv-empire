"The insurance company said my great-grandmother's portraits were 'just old paintings' and offered me $300 for both of them."

A Leesburg family called us devastated after a house fire in their historic colonial home. The "old paintings" were 1890s oil portraits of their great-grandmother and great-grandfather - the original settlers who founded their family's legacy in Virginia. The portraits had hung in the same spot for over 130 years, surviving the Civil War, two World Wars, and countless family celebrations.

The insurance adjuster saw smoke-damaged canvases with darkened varnish. We saw irreplaceable family history - the faces of the pioneers who built Loudoun County, their expressions captured in oil paint when photography was still rare. These weren't just paintings; they were the visual DNA of a Virginia family.

The Restoration: Through museum-quality conservation, we removed 130 years of accumulated grime, stabilized the original canvas, and restored the vibrant colors that revealed the strength in their ancestors' eyes. Today, these portraits hang proudly in their restored Leesburg home, where the fifth generation can see exactly where their determination and character came from.
