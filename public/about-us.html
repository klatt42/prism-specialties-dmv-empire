How We Work: Our Restoration Process

Transparency matters. Here's exactly how we restore your irreplaceable items from initial assessment to final delivery.

Step 1: Emergency Contact & Response (0-2 Hours)

What happens: When you call our 24/7 emergency line, a certified restoration specialist answers immediately‚Äînot an answering service. We gather critical information about the damage type, scope, and urgency.

Why it matters: The first 48 hours are critical. Smoke damage penetrates deeper every hour, water damage leads to mold growth within 24-48 hours, and electronics corrosion accelerates rapidly. Immediate response prevents secondary damage that can double restoration costs.

Step 2: On-Site Assessment & Documentation (2-4 Hours)

What happens: Our specialist arrives with diagnostic equipment (moisture meters, thermal imaging, pH testing) to assess every affected item. We photograph, catalog, and create detailed condition reports for insurance purposes.

What we look for: Structural integrity of art frames, data viability in electronics, fabric pH levels for textiles, paper brittleness in documents. Each item category requires specific diagnostic protocols we've refined over 25+ years.

Step 3: Restoration Recommendation & Authorization (Same Day)

What happens: We provide honest assessments of what's restorable versus total loss. You receive a detailed quote with timeline estimates. We coordinate directly with your insurance adjuster to streamline approval.

Our promise: We never recommend unnecessary work. If an item is truly beyond restoration, we say so. Our reputation depends on honest assessments, not inflated invoices. Average client savings: 30% compared to replacement costs.

Step 4: Controlled Environment Restoration (1-6 Weeks)

What happens: Items are transported to our climate-controlled restoration facility where specialists work in dedicated clean rooms. Art conservators handle paintings, electronics technicians recover data, textile experts process fabrics, and document specialists stabilize papers.

Our technology: Ultrasonic cleaning systems, freeze-drying chambers, thermal fogging for odor removal, laminar flow hoods for sensitive electronics, UV-filtered drying rooms for documents. Equipment investment: $450,000+ in specialized restoration technology.

Step 5: Quality Verification & Delivery

What happens: Every restored item undergoes final quality inspection using the same diagnostic equipment from the initial assessment. We verify smoke odor elimination, structural integrity, and aesthetic restoration. Items are photographed before/after for insurance documentation.

Satisfaction guarantee: If you're not satisfied with any restoration, we'll re-do the work at no charge. Our client satisfaction rate: 98.7% (based on 1,200+ projects 2020-2024).
