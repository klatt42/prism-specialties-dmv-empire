📋 Case Study: Confederate Officer's Sword Authentication & Restoration

Artifact: Family-owned Confederate officer's sword attributed to Colonel James Peterson, 15th Virginia Infantry, recovered from Spotsylvania battlefield area in 1920s.

Authentication Challenges:

  • Sword blade heavily corroded with illegible maker's marks
  • Guard and pommel showed period-inappropriate modifications
  • Family provenance stories contained historical inaccuracies
  • Previous "restoration" had altered original finish
  • No documented chain of custody since 1920s recovery

Our Comprehensive Process:

  • Electrolytic reduction revealed original "Leech & Rigdon" maker's marks
  • Materials analysis confirmed period-appropriate steel composition
  • Military records research verified Colonel Peterson's service at Spotsylvania
  • Genealogical research confirmed family ownership timeline
  • Conservation treatment stabilized corrosion without altering patina
  • Created comprehensive provenance documentation with battlefield context

Result: Authenticated as genuine 1862 Leech & Rigdon officer's sword, restored to museum exhibition standards, appraised value increased from $2,000 to $15,000, comprehensive provenance documentation provided.
