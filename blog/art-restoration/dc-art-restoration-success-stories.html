Case Study #1: 19th Century Oil Portrait - Smithsonian Institution

The Project: Water-Damaged Presidential Portrait

Challenge: A significant 19th-century oil portrait suffered water damage during a museum renovation project. The canvas had developed mold growth and paint layers were beginning to separate.

Our Solution: Our restoration team implemented a three-phase approach:

  • Stabilization: Arrested mold growth using controlled drying and anti-fungal treatments
  • Structural Repair: Relined the canvas using museum-approved adhesives and professional techniques
  • Aesthetic Recovery: Inpainting damaged areas using reversible restoration paints matched to original pigments

Results: The portrait was fully restored to exhibition quality and returned to display within 6 weeks. The piece now features enhanced UV protection and environmental monitoring.
