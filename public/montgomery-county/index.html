"The insurance company said my grandmother's wedding dress was 'just old fabric' and offered me $50 for it."

A Rockville family called us devastated after a basement flood in their historic Tudor home. The "old fabric" was a hand-beaded 1920s wedding gown worn by four generations of women - grandmother, mother, daughter, and granddaughter.

The insurance adjuster saw water-damaged fabric. We saw nearly a century of family history, countless memories woven into French lace and hand-sewn pearls. Each bead told a story of love passed down through generations.

The Restoration: Through specialized textile conservation, we restored not just the dress but a family tradition. The fifth generation will walk down the aisle in great-great-grandmother's gown, continuing a legacy of love that insurance could never replace.
